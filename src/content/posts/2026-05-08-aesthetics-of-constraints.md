The best creative work comes from constraints, not freedom. This isn't just a platitude — it's a fundamental truth about how human creativity works.

## The Paradox of Freedom

Give a designer infinite colors, infinite fonts, infinite layouts, and infinite tools, and they'll spend most of their time *choosing* rather than *creating*. The decision fatigue is real.

## How Constraints Help

When you remove choices, you redirect energy:

| Without Constraints | With Constraints |
|---|---|
| Choosing colors | Composing with two tones |
| Picking fonts | Mastering one typeface |
| Adding effects | Perfecting spacing |
| Following trends | Creating something unique |

## Applied to Code

The same principle applies to programming:

- **Language constraints** → deeper understanding of fundamentals
- **No-library constraint** → better grasp of the platform
- **Performance constraints** → more creative algorithms
- **Memory constraints** → elegant data structures

## The 1bit Philosophy

This blog exists as proof of concept. Two colors. No frameworks. No build step. The constraints didn't limit what could be built — they *defined* what makes it special.

> Every limitation is a design decision waiting to be embraced.

The most interesting things happen at the edges of what's possible within a constraint, not in the infinite middle of what's easy.
